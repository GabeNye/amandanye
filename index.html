WHO AM I?

It’s junior year of college. Or it’s your first “real job.” Maybe your first child. Every new deadline, relationship, or social expectation dumps another heap of anxiety into your lap. Pressure builds and depression is close by. It’s hard to talk to someone about it; they might not be trustworthy, they might not understand. Trying to keep it all together might be the easier route, especially if you have some help: pills to improve your abilities and drinks to keep calm during the stress. Substance abuse sounds like a heavy, clinical term. (“I don’t have that problem, that isn’t what this is.”) But once these behaviors start, it’s hard to make them stop. I want to be there for you when it all becomes too much to handle. I want to help you create boundaries and understand the disease you could be facing.

ARE WE A GOOD FIT?

Who is a good fit for my practice?

Finding the right therapist to fit you is important! I notice I work best with clients with these issues:

  • Peer or romantic relationship challenges
  • Anxiety
  • Depression
  • Substance abuse
  • Pregnancy, postpartum
  • Life transitions
  • Work or academic stress
  • Identity

WHO IS NOT A GOOD FIT FOR MY PRACTICE?

Because of the limits of my private practice, people struggling with these specific issues may be better served elsewhere:

  • High risk for suicide or serious self-harm
  • History of psychiatric inpatient hospitalization
  • History of suicidal thoughts, attempts, or self harm
  • Severe eating disorders
  • Anyone requiring more than weekly contact with their therapist
  • Anyone under the age of 18

BECOMING A CLIENT

SCHEDULING APPOINTMENTS

I meet with most clients weekly, preferably at the same time each week. We can decide to meet more or less frequently as we go depending on the therapy goals. I offer 24 hour online scheduling and a client portal for your convenience, as well as teletherapy if you are struggling to make it into the office. A cancellation fee of $80 is charged if a session is canceled without 24 hour notice, except under exceptional circumstances such as hospitalization.
